# ALPHA Compute — Public Problemology

## Problem
Modern computational workloads are increasingly constrained by a small set of recurring
pressures: the cost of running large jobs, the energy those jobs consume, the wall-clock
time to a result, and the memory and data movement that dominate many pipelines. Teams
running simulation, modeling, and large-scale numerical work often find that one of these
pressures — not raw hardware — is the real ceiling on what they can attempt. iTrix begins
from the specific pressure a team names rather than from a generic capability list.

## Secret
The insight behind ALPHA Compute is that many of these pressures come from how the
computation is organized, not only from the hardware it runs on. Reordering and
restructuring the work so that redundant movement and wasted precision are removed can
relieve a bottleneck that looks, at first, like it needs more machines. This is a
qualitative claim about approach; whether it helps a given workload is confirmed by an
evaluation, never asserted in advance.

## Solution
ALPHA Compute is an approach to relieving computational bottlenecks by restructuring how
eligible workloads execute. Where a workload is a good fit, the approach can reduce the
pressure a team named; where it is not, an honest evaluation says so. The solution is
described publicly in qualitative terms — the mapping engine and the routing math stay
behind an NDA.

## Product
In practice, a team works with ALPHA Compute in the form that matches their stack and
integration path. The public description covers what the product does at a functional
level and where it fits in a typical pipeline, without commitments that have not been
earned through an evaluation.

## Purpose
iTrix builds ALPHA Compute to create durable capability for hard computation rather than
a one-off benchmark. That intent shapes how the team evaluates fit and how it licenses:
the goal is a lasting reduction in a real pressure, confirmed with evidence.

## Proof
Public materials describe the approach qualitatively. Quantitative evidence — how much a
specific pressure is relieved — is produced by a scoped evaluation on representative work,
which is the point at which qualitative fit becomes evidence a team can act on.

## Commercialization
ALPHA Compute is made available through evaluation-first engagements: a scoped assessment
establishes fit before any broader commitment. Commercial paths range from evaluations to
longer-term licensing, matched to the team's needs and confirmed after value is shown.
